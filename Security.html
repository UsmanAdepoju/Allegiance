Our Solution Helps:

  • Improve Security
  • Reduce Cost

If your IT systems have evolved by default rather than by design or haven’t been reviewed by a qualified third-party in the last few years, you’re not only missing out on performance opportunities, there’s a good chance you have regulatory risk and compliance exposure. IT systems are a constantly moving target for planned and unplanned attacks from both external and internal sources. And depending on your industry, adopting one or several cyber security management frameworks will give you easy to follow roadmaps, budget tracking and concrete recommendations to keep your team safe, up to date and accountable. News headlines are littered with stories about malware, ransomware, wire fraud, botnets, data loss, privacy breaches and lawsuits. This is the new normal since there’s no such thing as 100% security or 100% compliance; the rules are always changing. However, proof of due care, will always lower your liability and is best achieved with a managed IT security partner who can keep you on track.

Allegiance Managed Cyber Security Basics

Antivirus/Anti-malware – Protects your systems, software and data from damaging attacks

Cyber Security Awareness Training – Educates your team on the latest threats.

Dark Web Monitoring – Monitors your identity information on the Dark Web.

Encryption – Protects intellectual property and personal data.

Firewalls – Blocks unauthorized access to your systems and data.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) – Provides enhanced authentication methods.

Remote Access – Ensures your security extends beyond the walls of your office.

Security Information & Event Management (SIEM) – Provides real-time analysis and reporting.

Vulnerability Assessments – Identifies gaps in your security and prevents undesirable content from reaching your staff.
